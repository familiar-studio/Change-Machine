Credit

Outcome Improve credit score

Overview

Credit has become an integral part of everyday life. Without good credit, consumers can have a hard time obtaining credit cards, which makes certain routine transactions like renting a car or buying airline tickets quite difficult. Increasingly and disturbingly, employers are using credit reports as part of the hiring process. For all these reasons, it is essential that customers know how to obtain their credit report, interpret it, dispute negative information, and know what they can do to improve their scores. When it comes to credit, there is a lot of misinformation and confusion about what affects one’s score, how to improve one’s score, and how long it will take for a score to improve. The truth is that damaged credit cannot be fixed overnight. It takes time, effort, and patience, but with a well-designed credit action plan, customers can see significant score increases.
