Banking

Outcome Decrease the cost of financial transactions

Customer calculates cost of fringe and traditional banking services
  • Customer Gathers Three Months of Bank Statements

    Bank statements can be a helpful tool to detect spending leaks and set goals, which are discussed in the ASSETS MAP and GOALS MAP.

    COLLECT DOCUMENTS

    For customers who already have bank accounts, ask them to bring in three months of statements for all their accounts. You may find some customers have accounts that they rarely use and think are unimportant because they have very little money. Make sure customers gather statements from all of their accounts. To get a clear picture ask:

    1. What banks do you have an account with?
    2. What types of accounts do you have at each bank?
    3. Are there any other banks besides these where you keep money?

    WRITE IT DOWN

    As customers answer these questions, have them make a list of all of their bank accounts on the ACTION PLAN and then develop a strategy to obtain all the statements. You can also use the WHICH ACCOUNTS WORK BEST FOR ME? WorkSheet to get organized.

    USE THE WEB TO HELP

    For customers who don’t know where their bank statements are, suggest setting up online access for each account. Almost all banks offer this service for free and it is very easy to access statements once the customer has registered. Use the AUTOMATE YOUR BANKING WorkSheet to organize the online registration process.

  • Customer Calculates Annual Cost of Financial Transactions

    Helping customers see the real cost of their daily financial transactions is eye-opening.

    DON’T FORGET ABOUT FRINGE SERVICES

    If customers are unbanked or banked but still use fringe services, they should also track down receipts for their use of these services, such as payday loans and check cashers. If customers can’t locate the receipts, ask questions to gauge how often they use fringe services, so that it is possible to estimate the cost. Questions to ask include:

    1. Do you use check cashers? If so, how frequently?
    2. What does the check casher charge you to cash a check?
    3. Do you pay bills with a money order?
    4. Which ones and how frequently?
    5. How much do you pay for the money order?
    6. Do you use pre-paid debit cards?
    7. How often and what are the monthly fees? Are there transaction fees or any other costs involved?

    DO THE MATH

    Once customers have gathered as much documentation as possible, help them estimate all of the bank fees and/or fringe service fees incurred over a twelve-month period. Be sure the customer is including ATM fees, overdraft fees, service charges, check-cashing fees, money-order fees, and any other financial transaction costs, and record these figures on the Financial Transactions section of the MONTHLY INCOME AND EXPENSES WorkSheet. It is important that customers take the primary responsibility for doing the math here, so that they have a clear understanding of all the fees that they’re paying.

    STRESS IT’S NOT JUST DOLLARS AND CENTS

    When customers have estimated the amount of money spent on these transactions, remind them to also consider non-financial costs, such as time and energy. For example, paying bills with a money order and cashing checks at a check casher is substantially more difficult and time consuming than paying bills by check (or online) and receiving paychecks via direct deposit.

  • Customer Researches Banking Products To Reduce The Cost Of Financial Transaction
    Ultimately, customers need to decide if their current financial system is working to support their long-term financial goals. Getting the costs down on paper and researching alternatives will help customers gather information to make an informed decision.

    DISCUSS BANKING LIFESTYLE

    After customers have calculated the tangible and perhaps less-tangible costs of their current banking arrangement, explain how a few changes could drastically reduce the costs and time they expend each month conducting financial transactions. Explore what it would look like to change their banking situation.

    RESEARCH ALL OF THE OPTIONS

    At this point, armed with the WHICH ACCOUNTS WORK BEST FOR ME? and AUTOMATE YOUR BANKING WorkSheets, customers should research banking services to find lower-cost alternatives. Some sites to point customers in the right direction include:

    1. findabetterbank.com This tool asks customers a series of questions to determine the most appropriate bank account.
    2. bankrate.com Click on the tabs for “Checking” and “Savings” to conduct searches for every type of bank account imaginable and compare fees, minimum balance requirements, and other important criteria in one place.
    3. creditunionsonline.com This is a great site to locate credit unions.
    4. joinbankon.org Bank On is nationwide movement to help get unbanked individuals banked. It has links to programs in California, Connecticut, Florida, Illinois, Nevada, and Texas.

    Caution

    For customers accustomed to using check cashers, share the 5 FRINGE SERVICES TO AVOID TipSheet with their coach. If customers are hesitant about opening bank accounts because of judgments, they should review the PROTECT YOUR ASSETS FROM JUDGMENTS TipSheet.
