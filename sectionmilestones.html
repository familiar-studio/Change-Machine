  • Roadblock: Customer Has A Tax Lien

    Until recently, the IRS made tax liens very difficult to deal with. Now the landscape is much easier to navigate.

    GET ALREADY PAID FEDERAL TAX LIENS WITHDRAWN

    Once a tax lien is paid, the IRS will withdraw the lien if the taxypayer requests it. See irs.gov/newsroom/article/0,,id=236540,00.html for more details. The prior rule was that even after the lien was paid it would still stay on a customer's credit report for seven years, dragging down a customer's score. Customers should file Form 12277 (Google it or go to irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/f12277.pdf) to request lien withdrawal. In Item 11 on the form, customers should check the box for "The taxpayer, or the Taxpayer Advocate acting on behalf of the taxpayer, believes withdrawal is in the best interest of the taxpayer and the government."

    USE CREDIT REPORT DISPUTES TO MAKE PAID FEDERAL TAX LIENS DISAPPEAR

    The only way to get a tax lien off a credit report is to have it withdrawn. Then send the CRAs dispute letters with documentation of the withdrawal attached (see CREDIT MAP for more details) and the lien goes away. The IRS may not report to the CRAs that the lien has been withdrawn. The burden is on customers to let the CRAs know.

    CAUSE UNPAID FEDERAL TAX LIENS TO DISAPPEAR

    The IRS will also remove a lien if a taxpayer enters into a direct debit installment agreement (DDIA) to pay a tax lien. That means the IRS will withdraw the lien even before it has been paid. According to the IRS the lien "will be withdrawn after a probationary period demonstrating that direct debit payments will be honored." Customers can contact the IRS at 1-800-829-1040 to begin the DDIA enrollment process.

    WHY IT MATTERS

    These new lien procedures are more important because tax liens can cause significant credit score damage, as much as a 100-point drop. WIth federal tax liens, the key is to ask whether the lien has been paid. If it has, ask the IRS to withdraw the lien. To address unpaid tax liens, customers should enroll in DDIA with the IRS to begin paying off the lien, get the lien withdrawn, and off customers' credit reports.

    DEALING WITH STATE TAX LIENS

    Unfortunately, these new IRS procedures do not apply to state tax liens. If a customer has paid a state tax lien, the customer should contact the state tax authority and request lien withdrawal. If the lien is unpaid, the customer should explore repayment options with the state tax authority, and, once repayment options are arranged, inquire about lien withdrawal. Request that the state tax authority follow the favorable IRS protocols.

  • Customer Determines Whether To File A Tax Return

    Whether or not a taxpayer is legally required to file a return, review the requirements and possible benefits of filing taxes. Most types of income (wages, interest, dividends and self-employment income) must be reported and are taxed by both the federal and state agencies. It does not matter whether customers are citizens or undocumented, whether they live alone or are the dependent of someone else, their income may be taxed (see the People in the household and Money coming in boxes on the FORM "1040" TAX PUZZLE WorkSheet). You can reassure customers that taxes are not as complicated or intimidating as they may expect.

    ALMOST EVERYONE NEEDS TO FILE TAXES

    A taxpayer is not required to file if she or he makes less than a certain amount. Check out the table below:

    For tax year 2011, customers with income above the following amounts must file a tax return:
    Single: $9,500
    Married filing jointly (MFJ): $19,000
    Married filing separately (MFS): $3,700
    Head of household (HOH): $12,200

    EMPHASIZE THAT EVERYONE SHOULD CONSIDER FILING

    Encourage all your customers to prepare their taxes and make this an annual habit. Many of the individuals you will work with are eligible for refunds, since taxes have been withheld from their paychecks throughout the year. There are also tax credits specifically intended for low-income taxpayers and students. Many customers will never know if they're entitled to a refund unless they take the time to have their taxes prepared. Tax laws and customers' lives change frequently, so a customer can easily become eligible (or ineligible) for a refund without even realizing it.

  • Customer Identifies Where To File Taxes
    Customers that use paid tax preparation services are often accustomed to filing at the same place each year, and may not even realize that lower-cost options exist. The paid tax industry spends over $ one billion in advertising dollars to get customers to use their services. This doesn't mean all paid preparers are bad news, but be sure customers fully understand their options before filing.


    FILE FOR FREE

    Many customers may be eligible to have their taxes prepared for free at a Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA) site. Customers should review REASONS TO GO TO A FREE TAX PREPARATION SITE TipSheet

    EXPLORE LOW-COST OPTIONS

    If customers earn too much money to qualify for VITA sites, they should still consider using other community or e-file services, which have higher income caps. Using these services is preferable to dropping several hundred dollars by going to a paid preparer. Read more about free e-file services at irs.gov/efile.

    CONSIDER SELF-PREP

    There are many do-it-yourself options and for customers with basic returns, this is a good alternative to spending the fees on filing. Check out beehive.org or TurboTax for other online options.

Caution

Undocumented customers can and should consider filing tax returns. They often will owe money because they do not have taxes withheld throughout the year. The IRS and federal immigration authorities do not share information with each other except in rare circumstances. See 26 U.S.C. §6103 for details. Filing tax returns regularly may eventually be considered a positive factor for citizenship. Undocumented customers whill need to apply for an Individual Tax Identification Number (ITIN) using Form W-7 to be able to file. you can find the form at irs.gov/pub/irs-pdf/fw7.pdf. Due to recent changes in some states' laws, undocumented customers should consult with an immigration attorney before filing a state tax return.

  • Customer Prepares To File
    Explain to customers that while tax returns are filed around April 15, planning, organizing, and strategizing is a year-long process. Educate customers about organizing and maintaining accurate records, planning both income and expenses, and ensuring accuracy of withholding information and deductions. An accurate tax return depends on a lot of information. It’s critical that you help the customer develop a system for recordkeeping.

    GET ORGANIZED

    Encourage customers to organize and maintain accurate records. Review documents they will need to file this year using the FILE YOUR TAXES FOR FREE WorkSheet

    CREATE A FOLDER

    Help customers create a filing system for the current year so that as soon as tax papers come in, they have a place to put them. Paper clips and post-in notes can go a long way.

    OPEN ENVELOPES

    Customers should make sure there is nothing wrong with the tax documents they receive. Ensure that contact information, Social Security Numbers and any amounts on W-2s, 1099s and other tax documents are accurate and correct.

    REVIEW INCOME STATEMENTS

    It sounds obvious, but it takes a long time to have a W-2 and other tax documents reissued if it goes to the wrong address. Ask customers to review their current mailing address with employers, financial institutions and the tax agencies.

    • Earnings in the form of wages, salaries, tips, and any income reported on W-2 and 1099-MISC forms must be reported on a 1040. Filing a tax return allows an individual to reclaim any federal income tax that was withheld from paychecks, estimated tax payments, and prior year overpayments.
    • Self-employed customers or those who work as independent contractors, freelancers, or earn cash need to !le Schedule C. In addition to business income, they may be able to deduct business expenses and losses as well. The SELF-EMPLOYED? TRACK YOUR INCOME WorkSheet is very useful for customers who need to track their business income and expenses. Many people who informally earn money selling things or doing odd jobs may not think they have a business and business income, but they do. And that extra business income can help someone qualify for a larger tax refund in some cases. Cash earners should review PAID IN CASH? WHAT YOU SHOULD KNOW ABOUT YOUR TAXES TipSheet.
    • Under federal law, unemployment compensation is taxable. Some people elect to have taxes withheld from their unemployment benefits and others don’t. Those who don’t elect for withholding will have to pay taxes on the benefits they received. Unemployment compensation is reported on a 1099-G.
    • Check out the 1040 for other income that needs to be reported on the tax return such as gambling winnings, alimony, retirement or pension distributions, social security benefits, etc.

    GET BANKING IN PLACE

    Using direct deposit can be a good alternative to get refund dollars fast. For customers that are accustomed to getting Refund Anticipation Loans (RALs), review the 5 FRINGE SERVICES TO AVOID TipSheet and explain the absurdly high fees and finance charges on these loans. Remind customers that by direct depositing their refund into a checking or savings account, the federal refund will be arrive within 8-10 business days (state refunds often take longer). The earlier in the tax season customers file, the faster they’ll get their refunds. Get customers set up before tax season using the 6 EASY STEPS TO OPEN A BANK ACCOUNT TipSheet. Confirm routing and account numbers of customers’ bank account where they would like to have the refund deposited. Make sure the customer walks into the tax site with a canceled check or deposit slip for their account(s).

    REVIEW PREVIOUS RETURNS

    Ask customers to bring in documents and tax returns from the previous three years. If customers do not have past tax transcrips, help request them by calling IRS at 1-800-829-1040 and your state's general hotline. Your state may charge a few cents per page to copy the return.

    SCREEN FOR TAX ISSUES

    Examine IRS and your state tax department notices and communications. These should be a separate folder for any tax year that you are discussing with customers. Don't forget to remind them to keep the folders in a safe place (it is full of confidential information).

    IDENTIFY RED FLAGS

    Ask customers if they have ever expected a refund and did not receive it or the refund came back less than what was expected. Assess customers’ past year return to see if all appropriate tax credits or possible deductions were claimed.

    DETERMINE APPROPRIATE STATES

    Remember that customers who live and/or work in two different states may have to file returns in both states. Also, many states have companion tax credits to the federal ones.

Caution

  • Be on the look out for customers who are employed by a business but are receiving a 1099-MISC instead of a W-2. If you believe the customer is an employee and not an independent contractor, you should refer the customer to a Low Income Taxpayer Clinic (LITC) to determine whether the customer shoulf file Form: SS-8 to receive money back for all the self-employment taxes the customer paid as a misclassified employee. For more information about how to find a LITC near you go to http://irs.gov/advocate.
  • Self-employed customers do not have taxes withheld or money deducted for social security and Medicare as employees do through their paystub. These customers should make quarterly tax payments to the IRS to avoid a huge tax burden when they file their return. Self-employed customers can read more about quarterly payments at http://irs.gov/businesses/small/article/0,,id=115045,00.html or by doing a Google search for "Self-Employed Individuals Tax Center."
  • Check out the information in the FORCLOSURE PATH on the enxt page, to help customers that also have settled debts like credit card debt and have received a 1099-C.
  • If customers have not filed recently and you think they might be eligible for a tax refund or may owe, you should encourage them to go to a VITA site to file the returns. Customers can go back three years when claiming refunds. Customers who owe money will be charged penalties and interest, and the longer they wait, the more expensive it will become. Some VITA sites allow for year-round tax preparation. Call 1-800-906-9887 to find VITA sites near you.
